Journey to Cracking the Interview of JetBlue Airlines.

Ravi belonged to a small town from India and he came to Arizona State University for his undergraduate in Computer Science. He had just completed his sophomore year and was given an opportunity to interview at a company called JetBlue airlines. He was interviewed for a position of Data Analyst intern at JetBlue. For his internship he had a couple of interview rounds in which the first round was to give a small comparison to the hiring manager with respect to JetBlue and its nearest market competitor using which was freely available for him to compile and give Tableau. So he arranged the dataset from the bureau of transportation statistics (BTS) the recruiters a comparison between JetBlue and their nearest competitor. He had recently taken a Data Visualization course and had learnt the basic concepts of Tableau. He explored the datasets on Kaggle but could not find an appropriate one for sending it to the hiring manager. He found the alternate link for the dataset here . He started the exploratory process with one of the most common question.

Q1. Which all destinations do JetBlue fly across the United States?

Fig 1. Plot showing the various destinations covered by JetBlue.

A1. He created this geographical chart showing multiple cities and states which showed the destinations and the origins covered by JetBlue airlines across the United States. Next, On exploring the source and destination he wanted to dig in the dataset and find more about the operating days and number of airlines for a particular week.

His next exploratory task was to determine the number of operating flights on each day of the Week.

Q2. How many flights did JetBlue had on each day of the Week in an entire financial year?

Fig 2. Plot showing the number of flights per day for a financial year.

On plotting the curve for the number of flights JetBlue had each day.Ravi realized that the number of flights were highest on Fridays and in general Saturday had the least number of flights.

Q3. Identifying the nearest competitor and comparing its industrial share with others.

A3. On exploring the dataset, he found that the nearest competitor of JetBlue was ExpressJet in terms of revenue and market share. He again drew the chart and realized that the total number of flights that ExpressJet had was around 400k and JetBlue had 300k. The origins served by ExpressJet was more than of JetBlue. JetBlue had 68 destinations across US whereas ExpressJet had 180 airports.

Fig 3. Overview of JetBlue with the others in the market in terms of total number of Flights and Origins served.

Summary

To summarize Ravi created an analysis of Jetblue airlines using the BTS Dataset and submitted the document which the company requested for his first round of interview. He found more about the destinations as well as the source of JetBlue Airlines. He also found weekly frequency of the airlines and concluded that the least number of airlines are on Saturday. Apart from that he also found out that their nearest competitor in terms of number of flights are ExpressJet airlines.
